Scratch from MIT
Scratch from MIT gives students the opportunity to delve into coding and learn core concepts by having them develop their own projects. These projects can be stories or short animated clips, and it exposes young people to the basic core concepts within all coding languages.

Google's CS First Tool
Google’s Computer Science First Tool includes free curriculum for educators to teach students how to code. These resources pinpoint educational activities for students that are rooted in learning about art, design, and storytelling. This resource is an example of what Computer Science Unplugged pedagogy can look like, especially if students don’t have access to computers.


This project is for Queering Education, taught at the Harvard Graduate School of Education.
